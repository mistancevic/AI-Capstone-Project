## The (g) split — first direct answer to the decisive open question

What people actually do right after the plan breaks mid-day
(estimated corpus share, not counted):

| Post-disruption behavior | Share | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Catastrophic relapse (bingeing)** | ~45–50% | Eats the untrackable meal, declares the day ruined, intentionally overconsumes until midnight ("well, today's ruined. I'll start fresh tomorrow"). |
| **Pragmatic adaptation** | ~30–35% | Eats the meal, logs a placeholder estimate, lightens dinner — "stop the bleeding." |
| **Punitive restriction / skipping** | ~15–20% | Skips out of tracking anxiety, or eats and then starves through the evening to compensate. |

Reading: **binge is modal, adaptation is second, skip is a real minority.**
This is the 3rd+ channel converging on the abstinence-violation-effect
mechanism (community names: "what the hell" / "f*** it" effect) — a minor
300-kcal lunch surplus becomes a 2,000-kcal day because the *belief that
the day is ruined*, not the surplus itself, drives the damage. The
~30–35% adapters are doing manually, with guesswork, exactly what
PlateMate automates — evidence the behavior is learnable and wanted.
All three behaviors are answered by the same intervention: the next
plan-consistent action with the "day still works" math.

## Disconfirming evidence — strategic skipping (the report's strongest correction)

For this persona, skipping a mid-day meal is frequently an **intentional
optimization**, not a failure: IF/OMAD to reclaim the lunch hour for deep
work; volume eating (banking a 1,200–1,500 kcal budget for one satisfying
dinner instead of three miserable mini-meals); declaring the office a
fasting window to delete tracking friction entirely.

> "Treating all skipped meals as user-journey failures will actively
> alienate a core segment of the target demographic who view the skipped
> lunch as a personal optimization victory."

**New design requirement (Develop backlog):** the skipped-meals safety
counter must distinguish **declared/strategic skips** (announced fasting
window, pre-planned banking — counts as knowledge, not risk) from
**unexplained or punitive skips** (post-guilt starving, tracking-anxiety
freeze — the pattern the safety tiers exist for). Connects to pause mode
and to the "counters count knowledge, not silence" principle; prevents
false-positive escalations on IF users. Also a coach-agreement item: the
coach decides whether the client's plan permits fasting windows.

## Product implications the run surfaced (all align with existing design)

1. Lower the cognitive stakes of estimation: placeholder entries, visual
   portions — PlateMate goes further (it computes the answer).
2. Reassurance that normalizes minor deviations, blocking the
   "ruined-day" belief — the multi-day averaging line and
   pivot-as-success framing are exactly this, now 4-channel-backed.
3. Never mark a repeated meal log or an intentional skip as a warning —
   monotony and strategic skipping are protective coping for this
   segment (reinforces rigidity-as-feature from investigation 1).
4. "Facilitate strategic imperfection" — the run's phrase for what the
   product should do; usable in the presentation.
