## New findings (beyond investigations 1–2)

1. **Skipping is structurally unmeasured, not disproven.** The field's
   standard lapse instrument codes three behaviors — unintended food,
   unintended time, larger portion — and has **no skip category**. Prior
   "contradictions" of our skip mechanism mean "unsupported by instruments
   that cannot see it," not "disproven." The skip-vs-binge-vs-adapt split
   can only come from primary data: real threads, survey Q3, coach
   interviews.
2. **Social events & travel may be the true deepest valley.** Party food
   2.9/7 vs packaged 6.5/7 is the largest measured gap in any dataset;
   socializing raises momentary lapse odds; r/loseit vacation mentions
   predict less weight loss (β=−.108, p=.005, n=477k posts). Recommendation
   on the table: social deeper than −10, possibly −25/−30.
3. **Synthesis (ours): the disruption moment most often wears social
   clothing.** Lapses cluster at home / evenings / weekends and social
   contexts; the "surprise colleague dinner" trigger sits at the
   intersection of handle-disruptions and social-events. Our flagship demo
   scenario (off-plan snack + colleague dinner tonight) is aligned with
   where the evidence locates the pain. Map v2 should treat the two valleys
   as adjacent expressions of one "life happens" super-valley rather than
   flip their ranks mechanically.
4. **Logging is a cliff, not a valley** (3rd source pattern): 3% one-week
   survival of 190k downloads; 94 of 141 journalers lapsed; revise −12
   toward −30 in map v2.
5. **Guilt-claims discipline:** compensation-after-lapse appears only as an
   untested speculation; one study found lower self-criticism predicted
   another same-day lapse (some self-criticism may be protective within-day).
   Our banned-compensation-language rules stand on safety/ED-harm grounds —
   never claim proven adherence gains from guilt reduction.
6. **Plateau dynamics quantified:** lapse frequency is curvilinear
   (4.22/wk → 3.86 → 4.63 at end of treatment) — motivational degradation
   late in programs; supports −15 estimate.
7. **Lapse-prediction feasibility note (future watch features):** ML lapse
   prediction reached 0.72 accuracy at group level, generalized poorly to
   individuals, and needed ~4 weeks of personal data — set realistic
   expectations for any future predictive nudging.
8. **Population caveat (applies to most of the corpus):** samples ~83%
   female, mean age 44–52, BMI 33–37, clinical programs. Transfers to busy
   desk professionals with low confidence. Persona remains unvalidated by
   every secondary channel — only the survey and interviews can fix this.
